System Playbook Overview

Core Philosophy

Stacking probabilities for consistent growth and peace of mind.

This system is designed around finding high-probability moments in time where the odds are overwhelmingly in our favor. By combining market trend, market-leading stocks, specific technical structures (squeezes & EMAs), and an options strategy that only requires the stock not to crash, we construct a scenario where the "path of least resistance" generates profit.

Anatomy of the Setup

Visualizing the Bullish Structure & The Launching Pad.

A valid entry requires a strict technical structure. Use the controls below the chart to build the setup and understand how the indicators interact to signal a high-probability moment.

1. The Launching Pad

Stock must be trading at or just above the 21 EMA. If extended (2+ ATR above), no entry.

The 21 EMA acts as support. Price action above it during a squeeze is just "noise".

2. Stacked EMAs & Squeeze

EMAs (8, 21, 34, 55, 89) must be stacked positively (shortest on top). Indicates strong bullish backbone.

Squeeze Histogram must be Yellow (exhaustion of selling) or Light Blue (release of bullish momentum).

Strategy & Execution

Deploying the Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Put Credit Spread.

The core mechanism is selling an OTM Put Credit Spread. We place a bet that the established bullish structure won't completely fail. Our only requirement for profit is that the stock doesn't move significantly in the opposite direction.

Defines Max Risk

Goal: Sell strikes as far UNDER the 21 EMA as possible.

Requirement: Must still achieve at least a 3:1 risk to reward ratio.

Mechanism: Time decay (Theta) works in our favor. As long as price stays above the Sell Strike, the spread expires worthless, and we keep the premium.

Trade Management

Protecting capital, taking profits, and ignoring noise.

✔ Profit Taking

As soon as filled on entry, set a GTC (Good-Til-Cancel) order to buy back the spread at 80% of max potential profit.

"Don't be greedy. Once a spread reaches 70-80% max profit, holding for extra gains isn't worth risking all open profit PLUS original risk."

📊 Position Sizing

Risk 5% to 15% of account on any given position.

Size depends on: Market conditions, setup quality, recent P&L, number of open positions.

If current setups are struggling, hold off on new entries or cut "weak links" to free capital for better setups.

⚠ Exits & "The Noise"

The Golden Rule: Only exit if "something changes" about the structure.

The first step to losing bullish structure is a close BELOW the 21 EMA on the timeframe you are trading.

  • Any price action, selling, or chop taking place above the 21 EMA is meaningless "noise".
  • Do not get shaken out by intraday movements.
  • On down days, ask: "Has anything changed?" If not (still above 21), hang on tight!

Adjustments for Extended Markets:

If the general market is 2+ ATR above its daily 21 EMA, be patient. If you must enter a great setup, lower position size or add more time (DTE) to sit through potential pullbacks.
