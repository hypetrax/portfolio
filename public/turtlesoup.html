The ICT Turtle Soup Setup

A modernization of classic false-breakout strategies. The Inner Circle Trader (ICT) Turtle Soup targets areas of resting liquidity. It relies on the premise that markets are engineered to sweep stops before initiating true directional moves.

What is the "Soup"?

Originally popularized by Linda Raschke and Larry Connors in "Street Smarts," the Turtle Soup exploited the failure of the famous "Turtle Traders" trend-following breakout system. When retail traders buy a breakout of a 20-day high, smart money sells into them, reversing the price. ICT adapts this by focusing on Liquidity Pools (old highs/lows) across all timeframes, waiting for the algorithmic "Judas Swing" (the fake move) to trap retail traders before aligning with the true institutional order flow.

↑ Bullish Turtle Soup

Price drops below an established Old Low (sweeping Sell Side Liquidity), traps early breakout sellers, and aggressively reverses upward, breaking market structure to the upside.

↓ Bearish Turtle Soup

Price rallies above an established Old High (sweeping Buy Side Liquidity), traps early breakout buyers, and aggressively reverses downward, breaking market structure to the downside.

Anatomy of the Setup

Understanding the core components is critical. A valid ICT Turtle Soup requires a specific sequence of events to unfold. Explore the foundational concepts below.

1

Liquidity Pools

Areas where resting stop-loss orders accumulate.

Tap to expand +
Buy Side Liquidity (BSL): Buy stops resting above old highs. Hunted for smart money to sell into.

Sell Side Liquidity (SSL): Sell stops resting below old lows. Hunted for smart money to buy into.
2

The Sweep (Raid)

Price intentionally breaches the liquidity pool to trigger stops.

Tap to expand +
This is the "Judas Swing." It looks like a breakout to retail traders, inducing them to enter in the wrong direction, while simultaneously fulfilling the orders of larger institutions preparing for a reversal.
3

MSS & FVG

Confirmation of the reversal and the entry mechanism.

Tap to expand +
Market Structure Shift (MSS): After the sweep, price aggressively reverses and breaks a recent opposing swing point.

Fair Value Gap (FVG): The aggressive reversal leaves an imbalance (a 3-candle pattern with a gap). This FVG is the optimal entry zone upon a pullback.

High-Probability Confluences

A Turtle Soup pattern alone is not enough. The highest probability setups occur when the sweep aligns with time and higher timeframe narratives.

⌚

Killzones (Time of Day)

Algorithmic runs on liquidity are time-dependent. The probability of a successful Turtle Soup increases drastically if the sweep occurs during specific volatility windows:

  • ▸ London Open: 2:00 AM - 5:00 AM NY Time
  • ▸ NY AM Session: 8:30 AM - 11:00 AM NY Time
  • ▸ NY PM Session: 1:30 PM - 4:00 PM NY Time

HTF PD Arrays
